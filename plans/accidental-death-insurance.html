Accidental death & living benefits coverage

Life doesn't always follow the plan. Accidental death insurance and policies with living benefits add an extra layer of protection for the unexpected — covering accidental death and dismemberment, and letting you tap your own death benefit early if you become seriously ill.

Accidental death & dismemberment (AD&D)

Accidental death and dismemberment insurance pays a benefit if you pass away or suffer a covered serious injury — such as the loss of a limb or eyesight — because of an accident. It's an affordable way to add coverage on top of a standard life insurance policy, especially for active people and those in higher-risk jobs.

Living benefits you can use while you're alive

Many of our life policies include living benefits riders. These let you access a portion of your death benefit early if you're diagnosed with a terminal, chronic, or critical illness — money you can use for treatment, bills, or simply living life on your terms.

  • Early payout for a terminal illness diagnosis
  • Access to funds for a chronic or critical illness

Frequently Asked Questions

Accidental Death & Living Benefits questions, answered

What is accidental death and dismemberment insurance?

AD&D insurance pays a benefit if you die or suffer a covered serious injury — such as loss of a limb, sight, or hearing — as the result of an accident. It's often added to a standard life policy at low cost.

What are living benefits?

Living benefits let you access part of your life insurance death benefit while you're still alive if you're diagnosed with a qualifying terminal, chronic, or critical illness — so the money is there when you need it most.

Does this replace regular life insurance?

No — it complements it. Accidental death and living-benefit riders are best used to strengthen a foundation of term, whole, or universal life coverage. Your broker will help you combine them wisely.

Is accidental death coverage affordable?

Yes. Because it only pays for accidents, AD&D coverage is inexpensive and easy to add. Living-benefit riders are frequently included at little or no extra cost on the policies we recommend.
