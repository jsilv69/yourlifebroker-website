Affordable term life insurance for your biggest responsibilities

Term life insurance gives you the largest possible death benefit for the lowest monthly premium, for a set period — typically 10, 20, or 30 years. It's the simplest, most affordable life insurance available, which makes it the go-to choice for young families, homeowners, and anyone replacing an income.

How term life insurance works

You choose a coverage amount and a term length that matches your obligations — for example, a 30-year term life policy to cover a new mortgage and young children. Your premium stays level for the entire term, and if anything happens to you during that time, your family receives a tax-free payout.

Who term life insurance is right for

  • Young families who need maximum protection on a budget
  • Homeowners covering a mortgage or other large debt
  • Parents who want coverage until the kids are grown
  • Anyone replacing income during their working years

Frequently Asked Questions

Term Life Insurance questions, answered

How long should my term be?

Match the term to your longest obligation. A common rule of thumb is to cover the years until your mortgage is paid off and your children are financially independent — often a 20 or 30-year term.

What happens when my term ends?

When the term expires, coverage ends. Many of our term policies are convertible, meaning you can switch to permanent coverage — with no new medical exam — before the term runs out.

Is term life insurance really cheaper?

Yes. Because it covers a set period and builds no cash value, term life offers the highest death benefit per dollar — frequently just dollars a month for healthy applicants.

Can I get term life without a medical exam?

Many of our carriers offer no-exam term life with same-day approval. Your agent will compare exam and no-exam options to find your best rate.
