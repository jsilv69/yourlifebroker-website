Indexed universal life: protection plus growth potential

Indexed universal life insurance (IUL) is a flexible form of permanent universal life insurance that does two jobs at once: it provides a lifelong death benefit for your family, and it builds cash value linked to the performance of a market index like the S&P 500. It's a popular choice for people who want protection and a tax-advantaged way to grow money.

How an IUL policy works

When the index rises, your cash value is credited interest up to a cap. When the index falls, a guaranteed 0% floor means you don't lose cash value to market downturns. Over time that cash value can be accessed tax-free through policy loans — which is why many people use an IUL for retirement income supplementation.

Who indexed universal life is right for

  • People who want permanent coverage plus growth potential
  • High earners who have maxed out other tax-advantaged accounts
  • Anyone seeking tax-free supplemental retirement income
  • Those who want flexible premiums that can adjust over time

Frequently Asked Questions

Indexed Universal Life (IUL) Insurance questions, answered

How is an IUL different from whole life?

Both are permanent. Whole life offers fixed, guaranteed cash-value growth, while an IUL ties growth to a market index with more upside potential — protected by a 0% floor — plus flexible premiums.

Can I lose money in an IUL?

Your cash value is protected from market losses by a guaranteed floor (often 0%), so a down market won't reduce your indexed cash value — though policy charges still apply. Your agent will explain the details.

How is IUL cash value taxed?

Cash value grows tax-deferred, and properly structured policy loans can provide tax-free access to your funds — a key reason IULs are used to supplement retirement income.

Are IUL premiums flexible?

Yes. Within limits, universal life lets you adjust your premium payments over time as your budget and goals change. Your agent will help design a plan that stays funded.
