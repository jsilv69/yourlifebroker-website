Whole life insurance that lasts as long as you do

Whole life insurance is permanent coverage designed to stay in force for your entire life — not just a set number of years. As long as your premiums are paid, your loved ones are guaranteed a tax-free death benefit whenever that day comes. It's the most popular form of permanent life insurance for families who want certainty.

How whole life insurance works

A whole life policy combines three powerful guarantees into one plan. Your premium is fixed the day you're approved, your death benefit is locked in, and a portion of every payment builds cash value that grows tax-deferred year after year. Over time you can borrow against that cash value for emergencies, college, or retirement income.

Who whole life insurance is right for

  • Families who want coverage that never expires or needs renewing
  • Anyone who wants a premium that can never increase with age or health
  • People who want life insurance that also builds cash value
  • Parents and grandparents planning to leave a guaranteed legacy

Frequently Asked Questions

Whole Life Insurance questions, answered

Does whole life insurance ever expire?

No. As long as your premiums are paid, whole life insurance stays in force for your entire life and pays a guaranteed death benefit — there is no term to renew or outlive.

Can my whole life premium increase?

No. One of the biggest advantages of whole life insurance is that your premium is locked in the day you're approved and will never increase, regardless of your age or future health.

What is cash value?

Cash value is a savings component that grows tax-deferred inside your whole life policy. Over time you can borrow against it for emergencies, large purchases, or supplemental retirement income.

Can I get whole life insurance without a medical exam?

Often, yes. Many of the carriers we work with offer no-medical-exam whole life policies with same-day approval. Your agent will match you to the best option for your situation.
